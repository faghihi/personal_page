Bio

I'm a person interested in computer science and specially artificial intelligence. educated in both top ranked universities of iran, amirkabir university of technology as Bs.c student and Sharif university of technology as MSc student, eager to learn ans experience new technologies. a fast learner and innovative person toward variety of problems. interested in artificial intelligence, knowledge reasoning, HCI, software automation, machine learning and etc.

worked as technical lead manager and project leader at several companies.

Research Summary

My researches are mostly parts of courses I have taken at university. apart from university surveys and projects,i have mostly worked in knowledge reasoning and situation extraction methods. in almost all my independent researches i was eager to use machine learning techniques in combination with human knowledge understandable by machines to make a more completed knowledge repository through relevant areas.

Cultivating admiration in brands: Warmth, competence, and landing in the “golden quadrant”

Jennifer Doe, Emily N. Garbinsky, Kathleen D. Vohs
Journal Paper Journal of Consumer Psychology, Volume 22, Issue 2, April 2008, Pages 191-194

Abstract

Although a substantial amount of research has examined the constructs of warmth and competence, far less has examined how these constructs develop and what benefits may accrue when warmth and competence are cultivated. Yet there are positive consequences, both emotional and behavioral, that are likely to occur when brands hold perceptions of both. In this paper, we shed light on when and how warmth and competence are jointly promoted in brands, and why these reputations matter.
